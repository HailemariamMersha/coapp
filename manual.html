Welcome to the step-by-step guide to completing the Common Application. This guide is designed for international and U.S.-based students applying to U.S. colleges. Each step includes helpful links and explanations.

  1. Create a Common App Account

    Visit commonapp.org and click Start your application. You'll create a username and password to access your dashboard.

  2. Add Colleges to Your Dashboard

    Once logged in, use the College Search tab to find schools and add them to your list. Each school may have different requirements and deadlines.

    Explore colleges

  3. Fill Out Your Profile

    Complete your personal information: name, contact info, citizenship, language, family and more. This helps colleges understand your background.

  4. Report Your Education History

    Add details about your secondary school, expected graduation date, grades, and curriculum. If you've attended multiple schools, list all.

    Education FAQ

  5. List Your Activities

    Include extracurriculars, leadership roles, sports, internships, and community service. You can list up to 10 activities.

    Rank them by importance and use active, impact-oriented language.

  6. Standardized Test Scores

    Many schools are test-optional. You can self-report SAT, ACT, TOEFL, IELTS, etc. If you don't have scores, some schools won't require them.

    Test policy info

  7. Write Your Personal Essay

    Choose one of the 7 prompts to write a 250â€“650 word personal statement. This is your chance to share your story and voice.

    Use CoApp's Essay Feedback Tool to get AI or peer-reviewed suggestions.

  8. Answer College-Specific Questions

    Some colleges require extra essays or short answers. You'll find these in each college's tab after you add them.

    Customize your answers per school's mission.

  9. Request Recommendation Letters

    Invite teachers and counselors by email. You can track submissions on your dashboard.

    Recommendations & FERPA

  10. Review & Submit Applications

    Final step! Check each section for completeness. Pay fees or apply for fee waivers. Submit before deadlines.

    Fee waiver info
